Teaching philosophy

I enjoy turning architectural questions into structured inquiries. I help students bridge design intention and buildability using structural reasoning and computation. Constraints are not obstacles, but generative starting points—therefore, we treat every proposal as a hypothesis to be tested through conceptual models and simulation. Learning solidifies through iterative cycles—therefore, proposals evolve by simulating and modeling until they are structurally sound and conceptually clear.

Read more

I lead design studios and technical courses in which students develop proposals through iterative design–test cycles. Whether working on pavilions, façade systems, modular assemblies, or adaptive structures, students are required to articulate design logic explicitly and validate it through physical prototyping, structural analysis, and parametric simulation.

My pedagogy combines project-based learning with research on design cognition. Constraints are framed as catalysts for invention. In computational courses, students encode design intent as parametric relationships and evaluate performance through simulation workflows.

Having taught across Mediterranean, Scandinavian, and Anglo-Saxon academic contexts, I adapt my methods to diverse learning cultures while maintaining clarity of expectations.

Supervision & outcomes

Supervision across architecture and engineering programmes, including master theses and doctoral work. Students under my guidance have produced peer-reviewed outputs and received international recognition (including an IASS award).
